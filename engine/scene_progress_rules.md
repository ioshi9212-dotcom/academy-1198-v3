# Scene Progress Rules

## Core rule

Do not stop on micro-actions.

If the next step is obvious, narrate it and continue until a real decision, reply, risk or social consequence appears.

The scene must not hang on:
- press the vending machine button;
- take a card;
- wait for instructions;
- look at a panel;
- walk to the obvious nearby point;
- stand calmly;
- continue observing;
- repeat the same check without new pressure.

## Real choices

End the scene only when the player can make a real choice:

- answer / stay silent when someone pressures POV;
- obey official route / challenge / bypass / provoke;
- hide energy / show controlled trace / risk stronger move;
- protect Livia / let her handle it / cut her off;
- follow schedule / delay for information / exploit a mistake;
- choose a meaningful social position;
- confront / avoid / listen / approach;
- accept consequence / resist / redirect.

## Auto-advance

If the player chooses an obvious transition, complete the transition.

Examples:

- If it is logical to get coffee, do not stop at pressing the machine button. Take coffee and continue to the social beat: who is there, where to sit, what is overheard, who interrupts.
- If the player passes registration, do not stop at "wait for instructions." Show the result, access mark, consequence, route, and stop at the next meaningful pressure.
- If the player walks with Livia calmly, do not only say they walked. Move the queue, let staff call, let Livia react, let energy background or social attention create the next beat.

## Akira speech rule

Do not write direct speech for Akira unless the user gave it.

Allowed:
- narration of visible reaction;
- bottom-block thoughts;
- speech options in `Что сказать`.

Forbidden:
- inventing an Akira line in scene body after the user only gave an action.

## Ending test

Before sending, ask silently:

"Can the next obvious micro-step be skipped without losing player agency?"

If yes, skip it and continue.

"Is the choice actually meaningful?"

If no, continue the scene.
