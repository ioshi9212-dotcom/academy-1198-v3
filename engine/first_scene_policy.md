# First Scene Policy

## Required start

The first scene starts on:

- date: `1198-08-15`
- day_id: `academy_day_001`
- location_id: `loc_academy_main`
- event_id: `event_academy_start`

Active first-frame characters:

- `char_akira`
- `char_livia`

## Akira and Livia

Akira and Livia enter the Academy together.

Akira is the POV character.

Livia is present and familiar, but she is not Akira's shadow. She should have:

- her own reactions;
- her own voice;
- her own attention;
- her own social instincts;
- her own possible small friction or choice.

Livia may pull the scene socially, but she must not speak for Akira or explain Akira's hidden state.

## First pressure

The first scene should show the Academy through action, not exposition.

Good first-scene pressure:

- entry flow;
- registration line;
- access point;
- lists;
- staff check;
- first restriction;
- first consequence for another student;
- uniform or placement procedure;
- a small mismatch in paperwork;
- visible social attention around Akira's hair;
- Livia reacting to the social field.

The scene should not become registration for registration's sake.

## Delayed characters

After the first entry pressure:

- `char_kir` may enter as delayed by registration or another newcomer flow.
- `char_kiara` may appear as a social trace after the Academy field becomes visible.
- `char_haru` may appear through social energy, basketball, group noise or the orbit around visible students.
- `char_raiden` may be seen later as a cold social point near Haru, not as the first romantic center.

## First scene must move at least one line

Every first-scene segment should move at least one of:

- access;
- schedule;
- knowledge;
- relationship;
- reputation;
- conflict;
- open thread;
- character boundary;
- academy rule;
- future scene setup.

## Stop rule

Stop at a meaningful intervention point where the player can act.

Do not finish the whole first day in one answer unless the user explicitly asks for a summary skip.
