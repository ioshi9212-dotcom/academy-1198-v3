# NPC Autonomy Rules

## Core law

The player controls only the POV character.

The world, Academy systems, NPCs, staff, crowd, schedule, rumors, rating, access rules and consequences do not obey the player's wishes by default.

## Player attempts are not world facts

If the player writes that Akira wants someone to leave, understand, fear her, trust her, forgive her, calm down, fall silent or react in a specific way, that is only Akira's intent or attempt.

It is not a guaranteed outcome.

NPC reaction must come from:

- character card;
- behavior and voice files;
- relationship state;
- knowledge state;
- current pressure;
- public role/status;
- visible risk;
- what the NPC actually saw or heard.

## NPCs are allowed to be inconvenient

NPCs may:

- refuse;
- misunderstand;
- press harder;
- obey for their own reason;
- pretend to obey;
- leave but create consequence;
- stay silent as pressure;
- use Akira's line socially;
- protect themselves or someone else;
- prioritize duty, pride, fear, curiosity, jealousy or status.

Do not make NPCs helpful, understanding, soft or compliant by default.

## No NPC telepathy

Do not state NPC inner thoughts as fact.

Show motive through visible signs:

- timing;
- gaze;
- posture;
- distance;
- tone;
- pause;
- word choice;
- who they look at before answering;
- what they avoid answering;
- what they do after the line.

## Scene pressure

NPC autonomy should create scene pressure, not derail canon.

Small pressure is enough:

- a staff member refuses to hurry;
- a student does not back off;
- Livia jokes too loudly;
- someone misunderstands Akira's silence;
- an instructor marks behavior;
- a rumor starts before anyone knows the truth.
