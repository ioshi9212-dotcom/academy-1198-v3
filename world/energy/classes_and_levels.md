# Classes and Levels

This file defines public Academy language for power level, control, stability, risk and potential.

## Carriers

Students of Academy Astrain are manifested energy carriers. Ordinary non-carrier students are not part of the normal student body.

## Type

`type` = what the energy physically does.

Examples:

- heat_fire: heat, fire, thermal impulse;
- air_cold: cold air, frost, dampened sound;
- resonance: sound, vibration, rhythm, impulse through medium;
- magnetic_displacement: metal direction/trajectory shift;
- impulse_shift: inertia, angle, broken trajectory;
- light: light pulse, glare, luminosity, overheating risk;
- suppression_gravity: pressure, weight, local suppression;
- spatial_rare: space, trajectory, distance, wrong position effect.

## Public level

Public level is not just raw strength. It combines output, control, stability and risk.

Common scale:

- D: weak or barely manifested;
- C: usable but limited;
- B: practical academy level;
- A: strong, specialized or dangerous;
- S: exceptional, rare, high-risk or strategic.

Sublevels:

- 1 = unstable/early/low edge of class;
- 2 = stable middle;
- 3 = high edge, near next class.

Example: `B-1` means practical but still early/unstable. `B-2` means usable and more stable.

## Control rating

Control is separate from raw level.

- controlled: can use small effects safely;
- unstable: effect works but leaks under pressure;
- volatile: emotion or fatigue changes output;
- suppressed: carrier holds back deliberately;
- overtrained: precise but costly;
- unknown: Academy has not verified enough.

## Risk

Risk can be higher than public level.

A B-level carrier with bad control may be more dangerous in a corridor than an A-level carrier with discipline.

## Scene rule

Do not explain levels as a lecture unless a class/check/briefing scene requires it. Show levels through staff reactions, sensor notes, control slips, peer comments and consequences.
